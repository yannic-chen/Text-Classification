 Fed Remains DovishThe Fed delivered dovish commentary, saying it would continue to buy at least $120 billion of bonds each month until further progress has been made toward the Committee’s maximum employment and price stability goals.
The Fed already had committed to not raising rates until inflation exceeds its 2% goal even if unemployment comes down to levels that generally had signaled price pressures.
Changing the language around the asset purchases underlines the central bank’s commitment to seeing the recovery through from its coronavirus-era slump.
