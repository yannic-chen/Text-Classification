 (Kitco News) - Gold prices are struggling to push back above $1,900 an ounce ahead of the new year.
By 2022, the bank sees gold prices averaging the year around $1,900 an ounce.
With inflation expected to remain subdued through 2021, Tchilinguirian said that the most significant risk for gold in 2021 is the unprecedented investment demand that drove prices to record highs in August.
A mass exodus from ETFs in 2013 created the biggest drop in the gold market in three decades.
He noted that the gold market has one saving grace: a weaker U.S. dollar.
